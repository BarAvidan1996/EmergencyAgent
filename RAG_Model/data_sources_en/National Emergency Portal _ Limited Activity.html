Limited Activity

Guidelines for managing daily routine for towns defined as orange level (limited activity), during a rocket and missile attack on the home front.

Educational Activities

These guidelines apply to any institution that conducts studies, including early childhood settings (up to age 6), schools, daycare centers, special education, higher education, vocational training, classes, camps, informal activity and youth movements, summer camps and seminars,competitive sports activity within the framework of sports associations and unions for children and youth up to 18 years of age.

Educational activities are prohibited in the following frameworks:
Early childhood settings (up to age 6).

Schools.

Afterschool activities.

Special education.

Institutions of higher education, vocational training and seminars.

Informal education (including afterschool activities, camps, youth movements, and summer camps).


Please note: Select and prepare the protected space, be familiar with the time available to reach shelter (the time to enter a protected space), make sure you are familiar with the means of alert, and train others in the rules of Life-Saving Guidelines during an alert.

Gatherings and Services

These guidelines apply to gatherings of people staying in the same place at the same time, including places providing in-person service (such as shopping malls, entertainment shows, weddings, synagogues and cultural activities. Popular sports activities, gyms and studio rooms for adults aged 18 and above and to the services available to citizens.

Gatherings: in the Gaza Envelope Guidelines area, in the towns of: Nirim, Kerem Shalom, Nir Oz, Kisuffim, Eih HaShlosha, Sufa, Holit, Nativ HaAsara, Nahal Oz and Kfar Azza in an open area, gathering can be held with up to 50 people. Indoors with up to 100 people.

Public Services: In-person service is possible in a place from which a standard protected space can be reached within the time available to seek shelter. In-person service can be provided in accordance to the rules of the gathering. Check with the service provider before arriving.

Please note: The protected space must be selected and prepared, the time available to reach shelter (time of entry into the protected space) must be known, ensure you are familiar with the means of alert, and the occupants must be trained and drilled in the rules of life-saving behavior when receiving an alert.

Workplaces

These guidelines apply to all workplaces, businesses and commerce, including those providing in-person service and competitive sports activity within the framework of sports associations and unions for adults aged 18 and above, except for workplaces that attributed to the essential economy category as detailed below.

All workplaces - Activities can be held in a place from which it is possible to reach a standard protected space in the time available to seek shelter pending South District Command guidelines.

Workplaces with public reception work according to the gathering restrictions of this level.

Please note: Open and prepare the protected space, put up signs and directions to the protected space, be familiar with the time to reach the protected space, make sure you are familiar with the means of alert, and make sure it is possible to enter the protected space within the time available to reach shelter.

Guidelines for essential workplaces

Check if a workplace is considered as essential according to the Ministry of Labor.

Workplaces classed as essential as defined in the Labor Service Law and workplaces and services that are essential for the population, for the war effort and for the State's economy, work under the following restrictions:

1. Make sure means of alert are present:
A. Employees and mobile service providers - make sure the Home Front Command application is installed on your mobile phone and that location services are activated. Also make sure a manual loudspeaker system is available.
B. Employees and mobile service providers - make sure the Nofar or Nofrit systems are functional and connected to the loudspeaker system.

2. Make sure the protection is functional:

The integrity and preparedness of the protected spaces, and personal protection gear (as needed).

3. Make sure means of providing information are available:

Making online services accessible in places where gatherings can be reduced

Putting up guidelines signs in emergencies

Training employees to behave correctly when receiving an alert

Instruction the people in the areas when a new alert is received

Guidelines on working in open fields

Work with personal protection gear available and within reach as determined by the office or the organization.

When receiving an alert - employees must enter the most protected space possible within the time available to reach shelter.
If this is not possible, it is essential to wear personal protective gear, lie on the ground and protect your head with your hands.

Guidelines for a facility with public reception

Activity must be conducted and a waiting area for the public should be provided inside a building or in a place where both employees and the public can reach a protected space within the time available to reach shelter

If possible, online services should be provided to reduce gatherings.

Guidelines for a facility without public reception

Activity must be conducted inside a building or in a place where employees can reach a protected space within the time available to reach shelter.

Guidelines for supermarkets or food retailers

Activity must be conducted and a waiting area or the public should be provided inside a building or in a place where both employees and the public can reach a protected space within the time available to reach shelter

Public transport Guidelines

Reduce lines according to Ministry of Transport guidelines .

Field crew must carry personal protective gear with them, according to the Ministry's guidelines .

In central stations- passengers must wait in a room or in a space from which they can reach a protected space within the time available to reach shelter available to them.

Important pointers for public transport drivers

City buses - Upon receiving an alert the drivers must stop by the side of the road, open the doors and instruct the passengers through the speaker phone

"An alert sounded, enter a nearby building or crouch under the line of the windows and cover your head with your hands, wait for 10 minutes"

Inter-city buses - Upon receiving an alert the drivers must stop by the side of the road, open the doors and instruct the passengers through the speaker phone

"An alert sounded, crouch under the line of the windows and cover your head with your hands, wait for 10 minutes"

Train - Upon receiving an alert the drivers must slow down the train to 30 KmH until exiting the area where the alert sounds. The ticket controller must instruct passengers through the radio.

"An alert sounded, close the window blinds, crouch under the line of the windows and cover your head with your hands". After 10 minutes, continue traveling.

Guidelines for delivering hazardous materials

The delivery of hazardous materials must be reduced to a minimum. Every delivery must be pre-approved by the Hazardous Materials Branch at the Home Front Command.

Guidelines for medical centers, clinic or baby care facilities

In clinics where a protection survey has been conducted - act as in Green, Blue and Yellow zones.

Conduct activity inside a building or in any place where staff and clients (visitors and patients) can reach a protected space within the time available to reach shelter available to them.

Guidelines for hospitals and nursing facilities

If a protection inspection has been carried out- act in areas green, blue and yellow.

If a protection inspection has not been carried out -- Departments and critical departments much be moved from places that are not protected (like temporary buildings and top floors) to protected spaces, according to the following priorities:

1. A standard protected space.

2. An internal space within a building with concrete walls with as few external openings as possible and two ceilings at least one of which is made of concrete, such as basements, stairwells and parking lots.

3. An internal space within a building or a space with external walls with no openings, including two ceilings or more, at least of of which is concrete,

* Any diversion of hospitals from these guidelines - under the authority of the top medical authority.

Dialysis centers guidelines

Dialysis treatments can be conducted inside a building (a place with a concrete ceiling and 4 walls of blocks) only
In addition, preparations must be made so dialysis stations can be operated inside a standard protected space, when changing into Orange warning level.

External welfare departments for populations with special needs and day centers for populations for special needs in the community

Activity must be conducted inside a building or in a place from which the patients, staff and residents can reach a standard protected space within the time available to reach shelter that is available to them.

Important terms

Defense time - time available to reach the protected space (depends on the town where we are staying).

Standard protected space - Residential Protected Room (Mamad), Communal Protected Space (Mamak), Institutional Protected Space (Mamam), shelter (public and communal) only.

Structure - a place that includes a concrete ceiling and 4 walls made of concrete or blocks.

Open space - places that are not inside a building, including buildings of light construction, temporary buildings, caravans and buildings made of plaster or wood.

The most protected space available - the most protected space that can be reached within the time available to reach shelter.

Essential economy - an essential factory as defined in the Labor Service Law, as well as workplaces and services essential to the existence of the population, the war effort and the country's economy.
